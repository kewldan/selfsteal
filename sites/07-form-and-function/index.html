02 — The Studio
Good architecture is ordinary in the best sense — it disappears into the life it was built to serve.

We are not interested in spectacle. We are interested in the moment a client walks into a finished room and can't quite say why it feels right — the width of a threshold, the height of a sill, the exact grey of a poured floor at four in the afternoon.

Every project starts on paper and stays close to the hand: sections drawn at full scale, materials sampled on site, and a small team that sees a building from first sketch to final walk-through.

— Elena Marchetti, Founding Principal

05 — Process

How a project moves

Six stages, one continuous conversation — no hand-offs to a team you never met.

01

Inquiry

A conversation and a site walk. We listen, take rough measurements, and agree on scope, budget bands and timeline.

02

Concept

Sketches, massing studies and reference imagery. We test two or three genuinely different directions before committing.

03

Design Development

The chosen scheme resolves into plans, sections and material palettes, coordinated with structural and services engineers.

04

Documentation

Full construction drawings and specifications for permitting, tender and pricing with our shortlist of builders.

05

Construction

Regular site visits, shop-drawing review and problem-solving. We protect the design where it counts and adapt where it helps.

06

Handover

A snag list closed out, a building manual, and a return visit a year later to see how the spaces are being lived in.

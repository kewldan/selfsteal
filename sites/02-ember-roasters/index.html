Our Story

It started with one loud, stubborn roaster.

In 2014, Marisol and Teo Alvarez hauled a battered 5-kilo drum roaster into a former bicycle shop on Kindling Row and started roasting for friends on weekends. The neighbors followed the smell. Within a year the bikes were gone, a long reclaimed-oak bar went in, and the line out the door on Saturday mornings has never really let up.

We still roast everything ourselves, in batches small enough to taste and adjust as we go. We buy green coffee through relationships we can name — farms in Huila, Gedeb, and Cerrado we have visited, argued with, and learned from. Then we roast slow, rest it right, and put our name on the bag.

“We are not trying to be the biggest roaster in town. We just want yours to be the best cup on your counter.” — Marisol Alvarez, co-founder & head roaster
Brewing Guide

A better cup in four steps

This is the pour-over recipe we teach at the bar. Weigh if you can, but a kitchen spoon and a little attention will get you most of the way there.

01

Measure & grind

Grind fresh, medium-coarse like coarse sea salt. Start with 22 g of coffee for a single mug and adjust to taste.

1 : 16 ratio
02

Bloom

Pour 50 g of 205°F water over the grounds and wait 40 seconds. The bed will puff and breathe — that is the fresh CO₂ escaping.

205°F · 40s
03

Pour in stages

Add water in slow spirals to 350 g total, keeping the bed level. Aim for two or three gentle pours rather than one flood.

350 g total
04

Draw down & sip

Total brew time should land near 3 minutes. Too fast? Grind finer. Too slow and bitter? Go coarser next time.

~3:00 min

About the photographer

I make quiet pictures of a loud, beautiful place.

I'm Elias Vance — a photographer working out of a small daylight studio in Southeast Portland. For fifteen years I've followed the same weather, the same tidelines, and the same stands of Douglas fir, waiting for the twenty minutes when the light does something I've never seen before.

My work sits between documentary and painting. I shoot on a medium-format rangefinder and a battered 4×5 field camera, print everything myself on cotton rag, and resist the urge to make the world look like anything other than it already is. The result, I hope, is a photograph that feels less like a postcard and more like a held breath.

Prints from these series hang in private collections across the Pacific Northwest, and in the permanent collection of the Willamette Regional Arts Center.

— Elias Vance
